## Notes

This box is **VRAM-rich and GPU-bound**: 32 GB total across two RTX 5060 Ti (one on a PCIe **x1**
slot). Unlike the 8 GB box, a Q4-class quant + 128k KV **fits entirely in VRAM**, so decode is bound
by GPU memory bandwidth, not RAM — hence ~100 tok/s vs ~20.

**Recommended (sweet spot): `IQ4_XS` or `Q4_K_S`, 128k, fully GPU-resident** — flash on, KV q8_0,
MTP draft 4, threads 8, all experts on GPU (`cpu_experts=0`, `kv_to_gpu=true`). Ready-made preset:
[`presets/qwen3.6-iq4xs-dualgpu.json`](../presets/qwen3.6-iq4xs-dualgpu.json)
(`lms get unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-MTP-GGUF@iq4_xs`, apply the preset, load `@iq4_xs`). ~100–103 tok/s,
coherent output (verified: correct arithmetic + reasoning), 0.86 MTP accept on structured prompts.
IQ4_XS (~20 GB VRAM) leaves the most headroom for larger context; Q4_K_S (~27 GB) is marginally
faster. That's **~3.8× the as-found baseline** (27.4 tok/s: untuned Q8, 256k, parallel 4, spilling
~11 GB to RAM).

**The quant curve is steep here (opposite of the 8 GB box).** Because decode is GPU-bandwidth-bound
on the resident weights, smaller/faster quants win and the "best quality Q4" is *not* the fastest:

| quant | VRAM | tok/s | note |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Q4_K_S | 27 GB | 103 | fastest that's still solid quality |
| Q2_K_XL | 20 GB | 103 | as fast, lowest fidelity — only for max context headroom |
| IQ4_XS | 25 GB | 100 | **sweet spot** — imatrix 4-bit, best quality-per-byte, headroom |
| Q4_K_XL | 27 GB | 84 | UD keeps more layers high-precision → ~20% more GPU bytes/token |
| Q8_0 (hybrid) | spills | 33 | doesn't fit → experts stream from RAM (see below) |

**The x1 card helps full-residency but hurts hybrid.** With the model fully in VRAM, llama.cpp
layer-splits and only a tiny per-token hidden state crosses the x1 link (GPU1 ran Gen4 x1 at ~49%
util, no bottleneck) — so the second card is pure extra VRAM. But at **Q8 the model doesn't fit**, so
experts stream from system RAM, and any streamed expert feeding the x1 card is throttled: Q8 hybrid
managed only ~33 tok/s (worse than the single-16GB-GPU hybrid anchor of 45.9). **If you need Q8
fidelity, this x16+x1 split is the wrong tool** — stay at Q4-class-in-VRAM, or move the second card
off the x1 slot onto a wider link.

Levers that didn't matter here: `cpu_experts` ratio (irrelevant once everything's resident);
KV→GPU (always on, room to spare). Context is retunable later; 128k was the target and fits with room.

With MTP **on**, total decode is flat (~90–100) and just divided among streams — batching gains
nothing. With MTP **off**, total scales toward the MoE-bandwidth ceiling: 87 → 136 → 180 → 202, with
**diminishing returns** (+56% → +32% → +12%) flattening near ~200. Each stream is also faster under
contention than with MTP on. VRAM held ~22 GB through parallel 8 (no spill). So: **single user → MTP
on (~105 tok/s); multi-user serving → MTP off, parallel 4–8 (180–202 total, 25–45 tok/s per user).**
The old spilling was Q8 or big-context×parallel, not concurrency itself. (The 8 GB box stayed flat at
~20 total either way — RAM-bandwidth-capped.)
