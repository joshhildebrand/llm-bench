This box is **RAM-bandwidth-bound** (8 GB VRAM, DDR4-2400 quad-channel ≈ 77 GB/s). The model
can't fit in VRAM, so it runs hybrid: attention/router on the GPU, experts streamed from RAM.

**Quant curve is flat here.** Decode barely moves across Q4→Q5→Q6 (20.8 / 20.2 / 19.0 tok/s) even
as the file grows 23→27→33 GB, because decode is bound by the *active* expert bytes/token and the UD
quants' active-tensor delta between Q4 and Q5 is small. So **Q5_K_XL buys precision for ~free** and is
the recommended default (95% MTP accept); Q4_K_XL is the max-speed pick. Only Q8 (15.7) falls off —
at 40.9 GB far fewer expert layers fit on the 8 GB card. (On a VRAM-rich box the curve is *not* flat;
see the cross-machine note in the top-level README.)

**Full context (256k) works but with a catch.** It loads and decodes at ~21 tok/s, but the 256k GPU
attention buffer nearly fills the 8 GB card, so a non-trivial prompt OOMs and crashes the server.
Stabilizing 256k needs more experts pushed to CPU (`cpu_experts ≈ 0.78–0.82`) — fine for decode but
it **collapses prefill** (16k-prompt ingest goes ~3 s → ~55 s). **128k is the sweet spot**: full
speed, fast prefill, stable headroom.

| Context | Empty decode | 16k prefill | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| 128k, experts 0.70 | 20.8 | ~3 s (3700 tok/s) | stable, recommended |
| 256k, experts 0.70 | 20.9 | crashes (OOM) | not usable with real prompts |
| 256k, experts 0.82 | 21.2 | ~55 s (290 tok/s) | stable but prefill 18× slower |

Other levers: MTP draft depth 4 is optimal (past 4, accept rate falls); threads = 8 physical cores
(16 logical drops to ~16 tok/s); disable the F32 vision projector for text (frees ~1.8 GB, prevents
the Q4 128k `clip_init` OOM). Hard ceiling ≈ RAM bandwidth: 0.5 × 77 GB/s ÷ ~3.3 GB/token ≈ 23 tok/s.
