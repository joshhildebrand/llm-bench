This box is **VRAM-rich and GPU-bound**: 32 GB total across two RTX 5060 Ti (one on a PCIe **x1**
slot). Unlike the 8 GB box, a Q4-class quant + 128k KV **fits entirely in VRAM**, so decode is bound
by GPU memory bandwidth, not RAM — hence ~100 tok/s vs ~20.

**Recommended (sweet spot): `IQ4_XS` or `Q4_K_S`, 128k, fully GPU-resident** — flash on, KV q8_0,
MTP draft 4, threads 8, all experts on GPU (`cpu_experts=0`, `kv_to_gpu=true`). Ready-made preset:
[`presets/qwen3.6-iq4xs-dualgpu.json`](../presets/qwen3.6-iq4xs-dualgpu.json)
(`lms get unsloth/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-MTP-GGUF@iq4_xs`, apply the preset, load `@iq4_xs`). ~100–103 tok/s,
coherent output (verified: correct arithmetic + reasoning), 0.86 MTP accept on structured prompts.
IQ4_XS (~20 GB VRAM) leaves the most headroom for larger context; Q4_K_S (~27 GB) is marginally
faster. That's **~3.8× the as-found baseline** (27.4 tok/s: untuned Q8, 256k, parallel 4, spilling
~11 GB to RAM).

**The quant curve is steep here (opposite of the 8 GB box).** Because decode is GPU-bandwidth-bound
on the resident weights, smaller/faster quants win and the "best quality Q4" is *not* the fastest:

| quant | VRAM | tok/s | note |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Q4_K_S | 27 GB | 103 | fastest that's still solid quality |
| Q2_K_XL | 20 GB | 103 | as fast, lowest fidelity — only for max context headroom |
| IQ4_XS | 25 GB | 100 | **sweet spot** — imatrix 4-bit, best quality-per-byte, headroom |
| Q4_K_XL | 27 GB | 84 | UD keeps more layers high-precision → ~20% more GPU bytes/token |
| Q8_0 (hybrid) | spills | 33 | doesn't fit → experts stream from RAM (see below) |

**The x1 card helps full-residency but hurts hybrid.** With the model fully in VRAM, llama.cpp
layer-splits and only a tiny per-token hidden state crosses the x1 link (GPU1 ran Gen4 x1 at ~49%
util, no bottleneck) — so the second card is pure extra VRAM. But at **Q8 the model doesn't fit**, so
experts stream from system RAM, and any streamed expert feeding the x1 card is throttled: Q8 hybrid
managed only ~33 tok/s (worse than the single-16GB-GPU hybrid anchor of 45.9). **If you need Q8
fidelity, this x16+x1 split is the wrong tool** — stay at Q4-class-in-VRAM, or move the second card
off the x1 slot onto a wider link.

Levers that didn't matter here: `cpu_experts` ratio (irrelevant once everything's resident);
KV→GPU (always on, room to spare). Context is retunable later; 128k was the target and fits with room.

**Concurrency: turn MTP OFF to serve multiple users.** MTP is a single-stream win but caps aggregate
throughput under load — its draft/verify structure won't batch. Measured (IQ4_XS, 32k, parallel 4,
VRAM ~22 GB, no spill):

All figures are **decode tok/s** (same metric as the single-stream table): *total* = combined across
all streams, *per stream* = what each concurrent user sees.

| streams | MTP on — total | MTP on — per stream | MTP off — total | MTP off — per stream |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 105 | 105 | 87 | 87 |
| 2 | 89 | 44 | 136 | 68 |
| 4 | 100 | 25 | 180 | 45 |
| 8 | — | — | **202** | 25 |

With MTP **on**, total decode is flat (~90–100) and just divided among streams — batching gains
nothing. With MTP **off**, total scales toward the MoE-bandwidth ceiling: 87 → 136 → 180 → 202, with
**diminishing returns** (+56% → +32% → +12%) flattening near ~200. Each stream is also faster under
contention than with MTP on. VRAM held ~22 GB through parallel 8 (no spill). So: **single user → MTP
on (~105 tok/s); multi-user serving → MTP off, parallel 4–8 (180–202 total, 25–45 tok/s per user).**
The old spilling was Q8 or big-context×parallel, not concurrency itself. (The 8 GB box stayed flat at
~20 total either way — RAM-bandwidth-capped.)

## gpt-oss-120b (117B / 5.1B active, MXFP4 experts, 63.4 GB)

**Recommended: hybrid, `cpu_experts=0.61`, 32k ctx, flash on, KV q8_0 on GPU, threads 8,
`keep_in_ram=false`, `mmap=true` → ~21 tok/s decode, ~326 tok/s prefill (34 s per 16k prompt).**
Preset: [`presets/gpt-oss-120b-hybrid-dualgpu.json`](../presets/gpt-oss-120b-hybrid-dualgpu.json).
Coherence verified (arithmetic + factual). No MTP head; MTP off.

**Quants are a dead end for this model:** experts (~99% of bytes) are native MXFP4 and don't
requantize — the unsloth GGUFs run 62.6 GB (Q2_K) to 63.4 GB (Q8_0), <2% apart. Expert *placement*
is the only lever on this box.

**The cpu_experts ladder is shallow** (each ~4 layers ≈ 6.5 GB of VRAM buys ~1 tok/s), because the
CPU-resident experts dominate per-token time — decode stays RAM-bandwidth-bound at every ratio:

| cpu_experts | expert layers on GPU | VRAM | decode tok/s |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1.00 | 0 | 4.9 GB | 17.3 |
| 0.89 | 4 | 9.7 GB | 18.0 |
| 0.78 | 8 | 15.8 GB | 19.3 |
| 0.67 | 12 | 22.2 GB | 20.6 |
| 0.61 | 14 | 25.5 GB | **21.2** |

LM Studio **clamps at 14 expert layers** here — ratios below 0.61 (0.58, 0.55) load byte-identical
placements, so 0.61 is the effective floor. Concurrency (at 0.67, parallel 8, 32k): total decode
20.5 → 27.7 → 35.8 → 40.2 tok/s at 1/2/4/8 streams — ~2× at 8 streams, per-stream 5 tok/s.

**⚠ OOM post-mortem (this box hard-locked once on this model).** The crash config was
ctx=120000 + cpu_experts=0.583 + **no flash attention**: ~25 GB of experts + 120k KV + a huge
non-flash attention scratch buffer overflowed VRAM, and the CUDA sysmem fallback committed the
overflow into host RAM on top of ~35 GB of mmap'd experts — commit charge blew past RAM+pagefile
and Windows froze. Rules for 60 GB-class hybrids on a 64 GB box: flash **on** always; big ctx and
low cpu_experts are a multiplicative VRAM cost — change one at a time; `keep_in_ram=false` +
`mmap=true` so CPU-side weights stay reclaimable; run `ramguard.ps1` (commit-floor watchdog)
during loads. Note: free *physical* RAM near zero during load/prefill is normal mmap behavior and
harmless — commit headroom is the signal that matters.

**Where this lands:** ~5× slower than qwen3.6-35B IQ4_XS fully-resident (~100 tok/s) on the same
box. Usable for single-user chat/agent work where the capability jump is worth 21 tok/s; prefill
makes big-context agentic loops painful (34 s per 16k of fresh prompt; 128k would be several
minutes). 128k ctx untested — would need cpu_experts backed off ~2 layers for KV; retune if needed.

## Nemotron-3-Super-120B-A12B (hybrid Mamba-2 + LatentMoE, UD-Q2_K_XL 54.7 GB)

**Verdict: runs, coherent at Q2, but ~2× slower than gpt-oss-120b — 9.3 tok/s decode,
235 tok/s prefill (48 s per 16k) at `cpu_experts=0.57`.** The 12B active parameters (22 of 512
experts + 1 shared, 88 blocks) simply read ~2× the bytes/token of gpt-oss's 5.1B active, and on a
RAM-bandwidth-bound box that's the whole story. The expert ladder is even shallower than gpt-oss:

| cpu_experts | VRAM | decode tok/s |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 1.00 | 6.8 GB | 8.0 |
| 0.85 | 13.8 GB | 8.2 |
| 0.70 | 20.6 GB | 8.7 |
| 0.57 | 26.5 GB | 9.3 |

Unlike gpt-oss, this model IS quant-flexible (BF16-trained: 52.7 GB IQ1_M → 128 GB Q8_0) — but the
dense/Mamba layers set a ~53 GB floor, so no quant escapes hybrid on 32 GB VRAM, and Q2→Q1 buys
almost nothing. The model card advertises MTP, but the unsloth GGUF **doesn't bundle the MTP head**
(LM Studio: "requires a GGUF model with a bundled supported MTP head"), so no speculative rescue.
Not recommended on this box while gpt-oss-120b exists at 2× the speed in the same footprint.

## Qwen3-Coder-Next (80B-class MoE, UD-Q2_K_XL 26.8 GB — FULLY RESIDENT)

**The best big-model profile on this box: 59 tok/s single-stream, 117 tok/s aggregate @8 streams,
951 tok/s prefill (11.6 s per 16k), coherent at Q2.** All of it GPU-resident (26.3 GB across both
cards, KV tiny thanks to 2-KV-head DeltaNet hybrid attention; 256k native ctx). Config:
`cpu_experts=0`, 32k ctx, flash on, KV q8_0, parallel 8 for serving. No MTP head in the GGUF.

Notable: despite ~3B active (like the 30B coder that does 121 tok/s here), it decodes at ~59 —
the Gated-DeltaNet linear-attention layers cost real GPU compute per token. Still: a 80B-class
agentic coder at 59 tok/s with 951 tok/s prefill beats every hybrid (gpt-oss-120b: 21/326) on both
axes. Q4_K_S (45.5 GB) would need hybrid offload — untested; Q2→Q4 quality delta unmeasured here.

## Dense models on this box: the x1 link taxes every token

Two dense residents benched (32k ctx, flash, KV q8_0, mmproj disabled, MTP n/a):

| model | quant | size | decode | prefill | note |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Devstral-Small-2-24B | Q8_0 | 25.1 GB | 9.4 | 799 | Q8 dense = worst case: 25 GB/token across both cards incl. the Gen1-x1 hop |
| Tess-4-27B (qwen3.5 dense) | Q4_K_S | 17.6 GB | 21.9 | 1246 | coherent; no MTP head in bartowski GGUF (EAGLE3 draft exists, untested) |

Dense decode = total resident bytes/token, and anything split across the cards pays the x1
latency every layer crossing. **Devstral at Q8 is not viable here (9.4 tok/s); a Q4_K_M (~14 GB)
would fit on the single x16 card — expected ~2-3× faster, untested.** Compare qwen3.6-27B IQ4_XS
at 38 tok/s — that's what MTP (0.73 accept) adds on top of a ~Tess-class dense baseline.

Config gotcha discovered via Devstral: hub-resolved models ("publisher/name (1 variant)" in
`lms ls`) read their load config from `<publisher>/<model-key>.json`, NOT the physical gguf path —
apply_config to the wrong key silently no-ops and you bench LM Studio defaults (ctx 8192, flash
off). **Always verify `lms ps` shows your context length after load.**
